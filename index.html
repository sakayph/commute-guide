Walking

Walking is unavoidable in order to get around. When walking, try your best to stay aware of your surroundings.

Stay on the sidewalks and avoid walking on the road.

Keep your bag closed and your belongings secure during your commute. Be constantly aware of your surroundings to prevent your belongings from being snatched.

Look towards the direction of oncoming traffic before crossing.

Cross at a steady pace, but don’t dally. Taking too long will irritate drivers.

Don’t text or chat while crossing the street.

Jeepney

Jeepneys are the most common mode of transportation. They have fixed routes and are used for both short and long distances. These modified war vehicles are usually made of stainless steel decorated with bright and colorful designs.

Jeepneys usually have signboards on their windshield with their stops written on it. They also have their stops written on the sides of the jeepneys!

Extend your hand to hail your chosen jeepney. If you’re not sure whether the jeepney is passing by your destination, you can always ask the driver before getting on board.

After getting a seat, pay by calling out "Bayad po!" and reach out with your payment.

Say "Para po!" to halt the jeepney when you arrive at your destination. Some jeepneys have strings that you can pull on to signal the driver to stop.

Keep your belongings close to keep them safe. Try to cover the sides of any bags to prevent thieves from slashing them.

MRT/LRT

The Metro Railway Transit and Light Railway Transit cover long distances and provide a direct route to different places throughout the metro. This mode of transportation can get rather crowded during rush hours.

You can either line up at the ticket booths or the vending machines.

For the ticket booths, the fare matrices are located at the window. You can either choose to buy the single-journey card or the Beep stored value card. Prepare your fare before-hand.

If you’re getting a single-journey card, tell the cashier your stop, pay, and get your card. For the Beep card, simply ask for it and say how much you would like to load into it. Note that buying a new Beep card has an additional cost of P20 besides the load amount.

For the vending machine, You can either buy a single-journey card or reload your Beep card. Buying a single-journey card just requires you to insert money in the machine and press your desired stop on the screen.

To reload, place your Beep card on the scanner and do not remove it until the transaction is over. Note that the minimum amount to reload is P13. You can also get a receipt from the vending machine just in case anything goes wrong.

Go to the turnstiles. For the single-journey card, insert it in the slot below the scanner. For the Beep cards, you can just tap them on the scanner above. Do not leave it there; you should get it back!

Line up behind the yellow brackets. When boarding, stand at the sides to give way to alighting passengers.

Board the train and find a seat. During rush hours, this is a race. If no seat is available, find a place to stand. Standing close to a door will help you alight faster.

Listen to the announcer for the next stop. When in doubt, check the back of the card for the map.

Alight at your stop and walk to the exit. If you chose the single-journey card, insert the card into the turnstile and proceed. If you chose the Beep card, you should tap it on the scanner again to exit.

There will be signs pointing you to your exit. If you’re not sure which way to go, the security guards are willing to help.

Bus

Buses are best for long-distance transportation. They operate not only within Metro Manila, but also traverse long distances to the provinces. The buses that have provincial routes have scheduled trips.

Buses have signboards with their stops in front. There are many signboards so try to start looking for your stop from a good distance.

Extend your hand to hail the bus. Board and try to look for a seat as fast as you traverse the aisle. If there are none, make sure you’re not standing in the middle so as not to block the way.

The conductor will approach you for payment. Tell him/her your stop and pay the fare. He’ll give you a ticket in return; make sure to keep it.

The inspector may board the bus and ask to see your ticket. Make sure to have it ready in a handy place!

The conductor will shout out the stops. Approach the door when you hear yours.

Tricycle

Tricycles are used for short-distance travel and can carry up to 3 passengers comfortably. It has two modes: regular, which would wait for the tricycle to be filled, and special, which would cater to you and your companions for a much higher price.

For regular: Get on the tricycle.

For special: Tell the driver your destination before boarding the tricycle.

For regular: Tell the driver "Para po" when you are near or at your destination.

For special: Give specific directions to your destination

Pay once you get off the tricycle. Don’t forget to slip in a "Thank you"!

FX

Originally known as the Tamaraw FX, the FX provides a more comfortable and expensive ride than the jeepney. Most FXs can seat up to ten people.

FXs have signboards in front with their stops written on it. They also have their start and end terminals written on the sides of their doors.

Extend your hand to hail the FX.

Hold out your payment to and call out "Bayad po!". If you’re seated at the back, you can ask others to pass your payment to the driver. As your payment reaches the driver, tell him/her your destination and the number of people you’re paying for.

Tell the driver "Para po" when you are near or at your destination.

If you are riding from a terminal, a conductor might collect payment before boarding. Tell him/her your stop as you pay.

Taxi

Taxis can take you anywhere in the city and the fare is based on a meter.

Tell the driver your destination before boarding.

The meter on the dashboard will tell you your fare.

If the driver isn’t familiar with your destination, guide him/her. Pay him/her once you arrive.

Take note of the license plate on the inside of the door. As a security measure, you may text the taxi’s plate number to someone you trust.

Always stay alert during your commute and make sure there is nothing suspicious going on inside the taxi. If there is, ask to be dropped off immediately.

Comet E-jeep

An E-jeepney works very much like a regular jeepney. However, it runs on electricity so it runs more smoothly and quietly. It also has a smaller carbon footprint than its counterpart, the diesel-powered jeepney.

The Comet E-jeep’s payment system uses a reloadable card. You can buy this the terminal booth.

The card comes with a set amount of load which you can reload at the terminal booth.

Tap your card against the sensor at the entrance of the E-jeep before boarding.

The Comet E-jeep has designated stops throughout its route.
